Step two:
develop add parts, change keys, make arrangements, invert chords, build it up. Does it need parts maybe it's just got a theme that just goes on and on. And it doesn't need multiple changes, or maybe it does. Developing the song does not have to mean it becomes more complicated each time it is played. It means that each time played it is becoming more of a unit, unique in its existence.

Change it up, how does it change the music or just keeps building the music along as you keep changing the chord configurations. Never be afraid to take suggestions. Others hear your song will have an idea for a verse or a way to change it up, it could be good probably, and you want to use it. Someone in the band wants to sing the song, but they have their own ideas about what the song is. That's fine. They can sing their verse, which doesn't have to be your verse, and it works out.
